# First Bites

![Visitors](https://visitor-badge.laobi.icu/badge?page_id=vipinkashyap.bffds)

**How the world feeds its babies** — an interactive guide to first foods across 35 countries, backed by WHO, CDC, and AAP guidelines.

[**Live site →**](https://vipinkashyap.github.io/bffds/)

## What's inside

- **Interactive 3D globe** with 35 countries plotted — tap any point to see that culture's traditional first foods, ceremonies, and sourced context
- **80+ foods** with age-appropriate introduction timelines (4–12 months), preparation guidance, and key nutrients
- **Top 9 allergen tracker** based on FDA classification, with filterable explorer
- **Month-by-month timeline** following CDC and AAP developmental milestones
- **Evidence-based guidelines** from WHO (2023), AAP (2024), CDC, and the LEAP study

## Sources

All food recommendations, timelines, and safety guidance are sourced from:

- [WHO — Complementary Feeding Guideline (2023)](https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/9789240081864)
- [CDC — Infant and Toddler Nutrition](https://www.cdc.gov/infant-toddler-nutrition/foods-and-drinks/when-what-and-how-to-introduce-solid-foods.html)
- [AAP — Infant Food and Feeding](https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/healthy-active-living-for-families/infant-food-and-feeding/)
- [LEAP Study — NEJM](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa1414850)
- [CDC Early Childhood Nutrition Report (2025)](https://www.cdc.gov/nutrition/media/pdfs/2025/CDC-Early-Childhood-Nutrition-Report-National-2025-508.pdf)

Cultural food data sourced from WHO regional offices, national pediatric associations, and ethnographic nutrition research.

## Tech

Single `index.html` — no build step, no dependencies, no framework. Three.js for the globe via CDN. Deploys directly to GitHub Pages.

## Disclaimer

This is an educational resource, not medical advice. Always consult your pediatrician before introducing new foods. Every baby is different.
